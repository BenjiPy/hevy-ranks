    How ranks are calculated

    Every muscle group gets one of nine ranks, based on your real training — aggregated across your top compound lifts, not on a single lucky set. Here is the exact method.

    The method

    Each set you log is turned into a comparable strength score, then aggregated per muscle group.

    1. Estimate a 1RM for every exercise. For each exercise, your best working set across all your sessions is converted to an estimated one-rep max using the Epley formula (reps are capped at 12 to stay realistic).
    2. Resolve the real load. Bodyweight movements add your bodyweight (e.g. weighted dips), assisted machines subtract the assistance, and pure bodyweight reps (abs, push-ups, pull-ups) use an estimated fraction of your bodyweight so they still count.
    3. Normalize to a reference lift. Each exercise has a coefficient relative to its group's reference lift (Squat for Legs, Bench for Chest, etc.). Machines and isolation lifts (calf press, back extension, pec deck…) get higher coefficients so their big loads don't inflate the rank.
    4. Divide by bodyweight. Strength is measured relative to your bodyweight, pulled from Hevy's measurements (or entered manually). Female standards apply a ~0.72 scaling factor.
    5. Aggregate the top 3 compound lifts. Per group, we take your top 3 compound lifts (those with at least 3 sessions logged) and average them with decreasing weights: 1.0 / 0.5 / 0.25. This spreads the score across your real training instead of hanging on a single lucky set.
    6. Match to a rank. The composite ratio is compared to nine thresholds per muscle group (see the table below). Click any group on your results dashboard to see exactly which lifts were used and which were excluded.
    estimated 1RM = weight × (1 + reps / 30)
    Epley formula, then per exercise: ratio = 1RM ÷ coefficient ÷ bodyweight, and per group: composite = (r₁ × 1.0 + r₂ × 0.5 + r₃ × 0.25) ÷ 1.75.

    Guardrails against gaming the score

    • Isolation exercises don't drive the rank when a compound exists in the same group. Calf press, back extension / hyperextension, pec deck, shrugs, most curls and triceps extensions are flagged as isolation. If no compound qualifies, the isolation lifts are used as a fallback and the rank is capped at Titan.
    • Minimum 3 sessions. A lift needs at least 3 distinct training days to count. Doing pec deck twice for a friend won't outrank your bench. If no lift in a group reaches 3 sessions, we still use what's there so you get feedback, but the rank is capped at Platinum.
    • Cardio and mobility are ignored. Only lifts with a real measurable load contribute to your rank.

    Thresholds per muscle group

    Minimum equivalent reference-lift 1RM ÷ bodyweight to reach each rank (male standards; multiply by ~0.72 for female). These are applied to the composite ratio, not to a single set.

    Good to know

    • One rank per muscle group today (Legs, Chest, Back, Shoulders, Arms, Core). Per-individual-muscle ranks are planned.
    • Your data never leaves your device — the API key talks directly to Hevy, and CSV files are parsed locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server.
    • Ranks are estimates. Coefficients and thresholds are tunable and will keep improving. This project is not affiliated with Hevy.
